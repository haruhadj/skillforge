INTRODUCTION
In today’s digital age, traditional teaching methods are often no longer enough to maintain students’ attention and motivation. With the rise of smartphones, video games, and social media, learners now expect interactivity and instant feedback. Educational institutions like Our Lady of Assumption College face growing challenges in keeping students engaged in subjects such as Math, Science, and Literature.

SkillForge was developed as a solution to this issue—a web-based educational game hub that brings gamification into the learning environment. By integrating points, levels, badges, and leaderboards, it transforms ordinary lessons into fun and competitive experiences that encourage active participation and continuous improvement.


INTRODUCTION
With SkillForge, students aren’t just absorbing information—they're immersed in learning experiences that are challenging, enjoyable, and tailored to their needs.


Key Features of SkillForge:

Accessible on both web and mobile devices
Real-time progress tracking and analytics for teachers
Personalized learning with engaging, interactive modules


PROJECT CONTEXT
Many schools, including Our Lady of Assumption College, still rely on traditional teaching methods that often fail to engage today’s tech-savvy students. While young learners are immersed in interactive technologies like mobile games and social media, classroom instruction hasn’t kept pace.

Gamification—using elements like challenges, rewards, and instant feedback—has proven effective in boosting student motivation, engagement, and learning outcomes.

SkillForge offers a solution by providing a centralized, game-based learning platform aligned with the curriculum. It personalizes the learning experience, allowing students to track their progress, earn rewards, and learn in a fun, interactive way.


PURPOSE AND DESCRIPTION
The main goal of this project is to design and develop SkillForge, a web-based educational game hub that boosts student engagement and learning outcomes through gamification. By transforming lessons into interactive experiences, SkillForge motivates students to participate more actively in their education.

Designed for Senior High School students at Our Lady of Assumption College, SkillForge is accessible on both computers and mobile devices. It integrates curriculum-aligned games with performance tracking to offer a fun, flexible, and effective way of learning.


PURPOSE AND DESCRIPTION

RELATED LITERATURE AND STUDIES
Local Studies

Dela Cruz (2023) explored how gamification can improve motivation, self-regulation, and academic performance in an online physics lab for engineering students. To address low performance in pandemic-era online classes, game elements such as leaderboards, coins, and level ranks were introduced into two test classes.
Using a mixed-methods approach (tests, surveys, learning logs, focus groups), the study revealed that students became more motivated and achieved better academic results after the gamification features were implemented.
    Key findings of the Study:
Increased student motivation and self-regulation
Improved academic performance through gamified mechanics
Positive student feedback on the interactive learning experience


FOREIGN STUDIES
Darad (2025) examined how gamification transforms both education and corporate training by enhancing learner engagement and knowledge retention. The study emphasized that integrating game elements like points, badges, leaderboards, and challenges leads to higher completion rates and more active learning.

It also highlighted the power of intrinsic and extrinsic motivation, showing that learners are more driven when their efforts are recognized through rewards and progress tracking. The integration of AI-powered tools was also noted for enabling real-time, personalized feedback.


LOCAL LITERATURE
Bagsic (2023) conducted a quasi-experimental study to assess the effectiveness of localized, gamified learning materials in improving the academic performance of Grade 11 students in Practical Research 1. The study compared two groups: one using traditional instruction, and another using gamified supplementary materials.
The results showed that students who used the gamified materials achieved significantly higher scores in both formative assessments and posttests, confirming the positive impact of game-based resources in enhancing learning outcomes.
    Key Points:
Gamified materials led to higher student performance
Study confirmed positive effects on learning outcomes
Supports SkillForge’s approach of using gamified content as an academic supplement


FOREIGN LITERATURE
Temel and Cesur (2024) investigated how gamification through Web 2.0 tools—such as Kahoot!, Socrative, Quizizz, and Mentimeter—affects students learning English as a Foreign Language (EFL) in online settings. Using a mixed-methods approach that combined quantitative and qualitative data, the study found that these interactive, game-based platforms significantly increased student motivation and academic performance. 

The results highlight the effectiveness of integrating gamified elements like quizzes and challenges in digital learning, supporting the use of tools such as leaderboards and achievements in educational platforms like SkillForge.

DESIGN OF THE STUDY

DESIGN OF THE STUDY
This study follows a developmental research approach using the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) model to guide the creation of SkillForge. The SDLC ensures each phase—from planning to testing—is carefully executed to produce a high-quality educational platform.

At the core of this project is a three-tier system architecture consisting of a presentation layer (user interface), an application layer (logic and processing), and a data layer (database). This structure allows for modular development, scalability, and effective performance tracking.


DESIGN OF THE STUDY
Key Design Elements:
Frontend: Built with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React.js and Bootstrap for responsive design
Backend: Node.js with Express.js for scalable server-side logic
Database: PostgreSQL for storing student data, game progress, and analytics
Web Server: Apache for stable and reliable content delivery
User Roles: Admin, Teacher, Student — each with distinct access levels and functions
